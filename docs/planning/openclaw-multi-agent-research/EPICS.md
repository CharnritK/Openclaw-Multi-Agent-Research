# EPICS

## EPIC-001, Planning bundle and operating rules

Objective: establish the canonical planning surface, scope boundaries, and execution discipline for the repo.

Why this exists: without a stable planning bundle, future research will sprawl and decisions will be re-litigated.

## EPIC-002, Framework fit evaluation

Objective: compare multi-agent frameworks and operating models against Point's OpenClaw-centered workflow.

Why this exists: the best-known framework is not automatically the best fit for bounded, approval-aware work.

## EPIC-003, Paperclip integration research

Objective: determine whether Paperclip should be used as a supervisory layer above OpenClaw and where its boundaries should stop.

Why this exists: Paperclip may add cost controls and operator visibility, but it can also duplicate orchestration responsibility.

## EPIC-004, Hermes integration research

Objective: determine the cleanest way to integrate Hermes Agent capabilities with OpenClaw without creating channel or runtime conflicts.

Why this exists: Hermes may be valuable as a parallel brain or specialist runtime, but bridge complexity can outweigh benefits.

## EPIC-005, Architecture recommendation

Objective: synthesize the research into one recommended architecture, one fallback path, and a prototype plan.

Why this exists: research is only useful if it ends in a concrete decision and next action.

## EPIC-006, Continuous research operations

Objective: keep the repo current through modular task execution, commit hygiene, and explicit next-best-task tracking.

Why this exists: the user wants ongoing follow-through, not a one-off document dump.
